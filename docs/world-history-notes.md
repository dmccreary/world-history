# World History Course Notes

For a world history course aimed at building college credibility while strengthening critical thinking, you'll want to draw from a layered set of standards: high-credibility frameworks that colleges explicitly recognize, plus pedagogy-focused frameworks that emphasize inquiry. Here are the major organizations to anchor your work.

## Tier 1 — Frameworks colleges recognize directly

### College Board — AP World History: Modern

This is the framework with the most direct college admissions credibility, since AP courses are designed as college-equivalent and many universities grant credit for qualifying scores. The course covers world history from c. 1200 CE to the present and is organized around themes including humans and the environment, cultural developments and interactions, governance, economic systems, social interactions, and technology and innovation. The framework has two essential components — historical thinking skills/reasoning processes, and course content organized into commonly taught units. The full Course and Exam Description (CED) is the authoritative concept list.

AP Central: https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses/ap-world-history
