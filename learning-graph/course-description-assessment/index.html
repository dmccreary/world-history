    3. Gap Analysis

    Minor Gaps (items scoring below full points)

    Analyze Level (9/10) - The seven Analyze outcomes are strong, but there is an opportunity to add one outcome focused on social history analysis — specifically how race, gender, and class hierarchies intersect across different regional contexts (e.g., analyzing how colonial caste systems differed across Spanish America, British India, and French West Africa). This would broaden the analytical frame beyond political/economic causation.

    No Major Gaps Found

    All other elements are complete and high-quality. The course description exceeds minimum requirements in most categories.

    4. Improvement Suggestions

    Priority 1 (Minor — Analyze level)

    Add one Analyze outcome such as:

    "Analyze how intersecting social categories (race, gender, religion, class) structured opportunity and resistance differently across colonial contexts in Asia, Africa, and the Americas."

    Priority 2 (Optional enrichment)

    The Topics Not Covered section is excellent. Consider adding one line noting that post-2001 events (the War on Terror, the 2008 financial crisis, the COVID-19 pandemic) are not formally tested on the AP exam even though Unit 9 nominally extends to "the present." This sets accurate expectations for students.

    Priority 3 (Optional enrichment)

    The Assessment section could note that the Long Essay exam choices span three different time windows (pre-1450 / 1450–1750 / post-1750) so students can expect to need evidence from at least two units per essay.
